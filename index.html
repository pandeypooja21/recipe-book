Breakfast Recipes

  • Besan Cheela

    • To make besan cheela, combine 1 cup of chickpea flour (besan) with water to form a smooth batter. Add finely chopped vegetables such as onions, tomatoes, spinach, and green chilies, along with spices like cumin, turmeric, and salt. Heat a non-stick pan and pour a ladle of the batter, spreading it into a circle. Cook on medium heat until golden brown on both sides. Serve hot with yogurt or green chutney.

  • Poha

    • To make poha, start by rinsing 1 cup of flattened rice (poha) in a sieve under running water until it softens, then drain and set aside. In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat, add 1/2 teaspoon of mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Next, sauté 1 finely chopped onion until translucent, then add 1 diced potato (optional), 1/4 cup of green peas, and 2-3 chopped green chilies, cooking until the vegetables soften. Stir in 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder and salt to taste, then gently fold in the rinsed poha, mixing well to coat it with the spices. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, then turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves and optional roasted peanuts for crunch. Serve hot with lemon wedges for a zesty touch. This quick and nutritious dish is not only satisfying but can also be customized with various vegetables and spices to suit your taste.

  • Paneer Bhurji

    • To make paneer bhurji, crumble 250 grams of paneer and set it aside. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee over medium heat, then add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter. Next, sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden brown, followed by 1-2 chopped green chilies and 1 medium chopped tomato. Cook until the tomatoes soften, then add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Stir well, then add the crumbled paneer, mixing everything together. Cook for a few minutes until heated through, then finish with a sprinkle of garam masala and fresh coriander leaves for garnish. Serve hot with chapati, paratha, or as a filling for sandwiches, making it a delicious and protein-rich meal

    Lunch Recipes

  • Dal Tadka

    • To make dal tadka, start by rinsing 1 cup of split yellow lentils (such as toor dal or moong dal) and cooking them in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water, a pinch of turmeric, and salt for about 3-4 whistles, or until soft. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil over medium heat and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Then, add 1-2 chopped green chilies and 2 chopped tomatoes, cooking until the tomatoes soften. Stir in 1 teaspoon of red chili powder and 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala, cooking for a minute to enhance the flavors. Pour the cooked dal into this mixture, adjusting the consistency with water as needed, and simmer for a few minutes. Finally, garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve hot with steamed rice or naan for a hearty and nutritious meal!

  • Mixed Vegetable Curry

    • To prepare a mixed vegetable curry, start by chopping a variety of vegetables such as carrots, peas, potatoes, and bell peppers into bite-sized pieces. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden brown. Next, add 1-2 chopped green chilies and 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, cooking for a minute until fragrant. Add the chopped vegetables to the pan, followed by 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, and salt to taste. Stir well to coat the vegetables with the spices, then pour in 1 cup of water and bring to a boil. Cover the pan and simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10-15 minutes. If desired, add a splash of coconut milk for creaminess and finish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice or roti for a delicious and nutritious meal.

  • Rajma Chawal

    • To make rajma chawal, begin by soaking 1 cup of kidney beans (rajma) in water overnight. The next day, drain and rinse the beans, then cook them in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water, 1 bay leaf, and salt for about 15-20 minutes or until they are soft. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown. Next, add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and 2 chopped tomatoes, cooking until the tomatoes soften. Stir in 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and 1 teaspoon of garam masala, mixing well. Add the cooked kidney beans along with some of the cooking liquid to achieve your desired consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Serve the rajma hot over steamed basmati rice, garnished with fresh coriander leaves, and enjoy this hearty and comforting dish

  • Channa Masala

    • For chana masala, soak 1 cup of chickpeas overnight, then boil them until tender. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, and sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden brown. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, 2 chopped tomatoes, 1 teaspoon of turmeric, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften, then add the boiled chickpeas and some water to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for about 10 minutes, garnish with fresh coriander, and serve with brown rice or whole grain naan.

  • Palak Paneer

    • To make palak paneer, blanch 250 grams of spinach in boiling water for a minute, then transfer it to ice water. Blend the cooled spinach into a smooth puree. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, and once they splutter, sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté until fragrant. Stir in the spinach puree, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric, and salt to taste. Add 200 grams of cubed paneer and simmer for a few minutes. Finish with a sprinkle of garam masala and serve hot with whole wheat roti
